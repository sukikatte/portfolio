FRANKENSTORIES

◆ Story Generation from Reusable Parts ◆

Final Year Project (In Progress) | Computational Creativity · Multimodal Narrative · LLM Story Generation
01

Project Overview

FrankenStory explores AI-driven storytelling through the recombination of reusable comic elements — text, panels, and visual cues — stored in a structured ComiXML repository. The project investigates how large language models (LLMs) can synthesize coherent, visually grounded narratives across sequential panels, aiming to create automated stories that feel intentional rather than fragmented.

02

Background & Motivation

Background & Motivation

Traditional AI storytelling lacks visual and narrative continuity.

This project leverages ComiXML, developed by the supervisor, to compare two generation methods:

XML-driven generation: Synthesizing stories directly from structured comic metadata.

Dialogue-based co-creation: Iteratively refining stories through conversational prompting.

The comparison examines which method better preserves story cohesion and character consistency.

03

Research Focus

Research Focus

The research targets three major issues in multimodal generation:

Hallucination: Inaccurate or irrelevant panel creation.

Cohesion: Weak narrative or emotional flow.

Integration: Misalignment between text, visuals, and themes.

It proposes new metrics for evaluating coherence, linkage, and creative fidelity.

04

Methodology & Implementation

Methodology

Framework: Python-based LLM interface + ComiXML dataset

Pipeline: Story concept → Panel retrieval → Scene synthesis → XML rendering

Evaluation: Human preference tests and narrative consistency metrics

The current stage focuses on implementing both pipelines, refining prompts, and assessing generation quality.

05

Expected Outcome

Expected Outcomes

AI-assisted storytelling engine combining XML logic with LLM creativity

Evaluation framework for narrative coherence

Dataset of generated stories illustrating different synthesis strategies

06

Reflection

This project enhances my understanding of computational creativity and AI narrative control, deepening my ability to connect software engineering, language modeling, and digital storytelling within a unified creative framework.
